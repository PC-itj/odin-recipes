Bacon Balsamic Deviled Eggs

Gourmet deviled eggs with a sweet and savory mix of bacon, onion, and balsamic vinegar. Always requested and always disappear! Can be made with sugar substitute and light mayonnaise with no discernable taste difference.

Ingredients

  • 12 large eggs
  • 4 slices of bacon
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup minced red onion
  • 2 teaspoons white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon celery salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Directions

Eggs

  1. Place eggs in a large pot in a single layer and cover with water by 1 inch.
  2. Cover the saucepan and bring the water to a boil, then remove from the heat and let the eggs stand in the hot water for 15 minutes.
  3. Drain and cool the eggs under cold running water in the sink.
  4. Peel and halve lengthwise.
  5. Separate the yolks from the whites, placing the yolks in a bowl.
  6. Arrange the egg whites with the rounded side down onto a serving platter.

Bacon

  1. While the eggs are cooking, place the bacon in a large, deep skillet and cook over medium-high heat.
  2. Occasionally turn until evenly browned, takes about 10 minutes.
  3. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate and chop

Finishing

  1. Mash yolks with a fork.
  2. Add bacon, mayonnaise, onion, sugar, vinegar, celery salt and pepper.
  3. Stir thoroughly combined.
  4. Spoon the mixture into the egg whites.
  5. Garnish with parsley.
